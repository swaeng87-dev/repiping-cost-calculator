Frequently Asked Questions

In the United States, whole-house repiping costs typically range between $4,000 and $12,000+. Factors include home square footage, number of stories, number of fixtures/bathrooms, and whether you select PEX or Copper piping.
PEX (especially PEX-A) is currently preferred by over 80% of homeowners due to its lower material cost, flexibility, scale resistance, and freeze tolerance. Copper is highly durable and proven over decades, but carries higher material and labor costs.
Most single-family home repipes are completed in 1 to 3 days. Water supply is typically turned back on at the end of each workday so your family remains comfortable.
Technicians make small, strategic access cuts in drywall to feed new lines. Professional repiping packages usually include complete drywall patching, texturing, and clean-up.
